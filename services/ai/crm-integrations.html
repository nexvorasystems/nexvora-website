The Problem

Why Disconnected Tools Cost Small Businesses So Much

When your business tools don't talk to each other, the cost isn't just time — it's accuracy, visibility, and missed revenue.

Data Lives in Silos

Customer information is in your CRM. Invoices are in QuickBooks. Appointments are in Calendly. Marketing data is in your email platform. None of these systems know what's happening in the others — so your team has an incomplete picture of every customer and every deal, always.

Duplicate Data Entry Is Everywhere

When a new lead comes in, someone enters it into the CRM. Then into the email platform. Then into the accounting system when a deal closes. The same information typed three times by three different people — with three opportunities for typos, inconsistencies, and outdated records.

Things Fall Through the Cracks

When one system doesn't know what happened in another, handoffs break down. A deal closes in the CRM but no one creates the invoice. A client books an appointment but no one is notified. A payment comes in but the project management tool still shows "pending." These gaps cost money and erode client trust.

What Integration Fixes

When your tools are properly integrated, information entered in one system flows automatically to every other system that needs it. Your team enters data once — or not at all, because it comes in automatically from a form or a trigger event. Everything stays in sync. Nothing gets missed.

How We Work

We don't just wire tools together. We map your data first, design the right architecture, and build integrations that are reliable and maintainable long after we hand them off.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What tools and platforms do you integrate?

We integrate with CRMs (GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Salesforce, Zoho, Pipedrive), accounting platforms (QuickBooks, Xero, FreshBooks), scheduling tools (Calendly, Acuity, Google Calendar), communication platforms (Slack, Gmail, Outlook), e-commerce platforms (Shopify, WooCommerce), payment processors (Stripe, PayPal, Square), form builders (Typeform, Gravity Forms, JotForm), and hundreds of other tools via Make, Zapier, or direct API connections. If your tool has an API or a native Zapier/Make connector, we can almost certainly integrate it.

How long does an integration project take?

Simple two-tool integrations — for example, connecting your website contact form directly to your CRM — typically take 1–2 weeks. Multi-tool stack integrations connecting 4–8 platforms generally take 4–8 weeks from audit to full deployment. The timeline depends on the complexity of data mapping, the number of tools involved, and whether any custom API work is required. Nexvora Systems gives you a specific, itemized estimate after the initial audit — no vague timelines.

What if one of our tools doesn't have a direct integration?

If a tool doesn't have a native Zapier or Make connector, we work directly with its API to build a custom integration. Most modern business software exposes an API. In the rare case a tool has no API and no native integration options, we'll identify an alternative in your stack that accomplishes the same function while being integration-ready — and we make that recommendation honestly, not to sell unnecessary work. We've never encountered a legitimate business tool that we couldn't connect in some form.

How do you handle data security during integration?

We use OAuth authentication wherever available, never store API keys in insecure locations, and follow platform-specific security best practices for every integration we build. All data connections are scoped to minimum required permissions — we request only what's needed for the integration to function, nothing more. We provide complete documentation of every access point and credential so you maintain full visibility and control over what is connected to what.

Can you integrate our existing CRM without rebuilding it?

Yes. Nexvora Systems works within your existing CRM configuration — we don't require a rebuild or migration unless your current setup has structural problems that would cause the integrations to fail. We connect your current CRM to other tools in your stack, establish bidirectional data flows, and add automation logic without disrupting what's already working. Businesses throughout Tampa Bay and Florida come to us with established CRMs and we integrate them successfully every time.

What's the difference between using Make, Zapier, and a direct API integration?

Zapier is the fastest to set up and connects the widest range of apps with minimal configuration — ideal for straightforward, reliable connections between popular tools. Make (formerly Integromat) handles more complex workflows with conditional logic, data transformation, and multi-step sequences that Zapier can't manage. Direct API integrations are built when no native connector exists or when you need maximum control, flexibility, or performance. We evaluate your situation and choose the right approach — often a combination — rather than defaulting to whatever is easiest for us to build.

What happens if an integration breaks or data stops syncing?

Every integration Nexvora builds includes error monitoring and sync failure alerts. If a connection breaks, you receive an immediate notification — and if you're on a maintenance retainer, our team is alerted simultaneously. All major platforms log every data transfer and flag failures in real time. We also document every integration completely so any issue can be diagnosed and resolved quickly, even by someone who wasn't involved in building it originally.

Can you connect tools that we've been using separately for years?

Yes — this is one of the most common scenarios we work with. Businesses accumulate tools over time, each added to solve a specific problem, and end up with a disconnected stack where nothing talks to anything else. We audit your entire tech stack, map every data flow that should be happening but isn't, and build the connections that unify everything. The process doesn't require replacing your existing tools or retraining your team on new software.

Do you offer ongoing maintenance after the integrations are built?

Yes. Nexvora Systems offers optional monthly maintenance retainers that cover monitoring, error resolution, updates when platforms change their APIs, and adjustments when your business processes evolve. Many clients choose to manage their integrations independently after receiving our documentation and training — and that's completely fine. For businesses that want ongoing support, the retainer option gives you a team that knows your stack and responds quickly when something needs attention.

How do I know which integrations will have the biggest impact for my business?

That's exactly what our tech stack audit answers. During the initial engagement, Nexvora Systems maps every tool you use, identifies every manual data transfer your team currently performs, and ranks each potential integration by time cost and error risk. The highest-impact integrations — the ones that save the most time or eliminate the costliest errors — get prioritized first. You see a clear picture of expected ROI for each connection before we build anything, so every investment is justified by a concrete outcome.
