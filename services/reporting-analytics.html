What We Do

Most small businesses have data scattered across multiple systems — a point of sale, a CRM, a bookkeeping tool, a spreadsheet, maybe a project management platform. The data exists, but no one has built a coherent view of it. So owners end up making major decisions based on whatever they can pull together quickly, which is rarely the full picture. The result is a business that reacts instead of plans.

Nexvora builds reporting and analytics systems that give small business owners a clear, reliable view of what is happening in their business on a weekly basis. We start by identifying the 8 to 12 numbers that actually drive your business — the ones that, if they move, tell you something important. These become your core KPIs. We then build the infrastructure to track and display those numbers in a way that is easy to read, consistently updated, and actionable.

KPI dashboards at Nexvora are not generic templates filled with vanity metrics. They are built around your business model. A service business needs to see utilization rate, average project margin, and pipeline value. A product business needs to see inventory turns, average order value, and customer acquisition cost. A subscription business needs to see churn rate, monthly recurring revenue, and lifetime value. We identify the right metrics for your specific situation and build dashboards that surface them without requiring hours of manual data prep each week.

Weekly reporting cadences are one of the highest-leverage changes a small business can make. When an owner sits down on Monday with a clear one-page view of how last week went across sales, operations, and finances, they make better decisions in less time. We build these weekly report formats for our clients — including automating the data collection so that the report is ready without manual assembly — and help teams build the habit of reviewing and acting on the information.

Financial metrics are often where we find the biggest blind spots. Many small business owners can tell you their revenue but not their gross margin by service line, their customer acquisition cost, or which clients are actually profitable versus which ones consume resources disproportionate to what they pay. We build the financial reporting layer that gives owners visibility into profitability, cost structure, and cash dynamics — not as an accounting exercise but as a management tool for running the business better.

Who This Is For
  • Business owners making decisions without a clear picture of current performance
  • Teams that spend hours every week manually compiling reports from multiple systems
  • Owners who know revenue but do not know true profitability by client or service line
  • Businesses growing fast enough that gut-feel management is no longer reliable
  • Companies preparing to bring on a business partner, investor, or lender
  • Operators who want to hold their team accountable to measurable performance standards
  • Service businesses where utilization, margins, and pipeline visibility are critical

Why Nexvora

Nexvora Systems was founded by Murat Zhandaurov in Tampa Bay, Florida to help small businesses operate with the same level of clarity and decision-making quality that larger companies achieve through dedicated analyst teams. We build reporting systems that are practical — not complex data infrastructure projects that require a data engineer to maintain, but clean, reliable dashboards and reports that your team can actually use.

We work with clients across all 50 states. Our reporting and analytics engagements happen primarily through video calls and screen-sharing sessions on Zoom, which allows us to build and configure dashboards directly in your tools and walk your team through how to use and interpret them in real time. Florida-based clients can also choose to work with us in person when that is more effective.

The standard we hold ourselves to is simple: every reporting system we build should save your team time, reduce guesswork in decision-making, and give you early warning when something in the business needs attention. If the reports are not changing how you manage the business, they are not doing their job.
