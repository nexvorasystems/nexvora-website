Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to build a website?

Most websites take 4–8 weeks from kickoff to launch. This includes discovery, copywriting, design approval, development, and testing. Timelines depend on how quickly feedback is provided and the complexity of the project. We give you a detailed timeline in our proposal so you know exactly what to expect.

What platform do you build websites on?

We recommend the platform that best fits your needs and budget. Most small business sites work well with Webflow, WordPress, or custom HTML/CSS depending on complexity. We explain the trade-offs of each platform in plain English so you make an informed choice — not whichever one is easiest for us to build.

Do we need to redesign our whole website, or can you just fix what's not working?

You don't always need a full redesign. Sometimes a targeted copy refresh, better calls-to-action, and speed improvements can dramatically increase conversions from your existing site. We audit your current site first and recommend only what's actually necessary. Full redesigns are recommended when the foundation (structure, messaging, or technology) needs to be replaced.

What happens after the website launches?

We provide 30 days of post-launch support included in every project — bug fixes, content updates, and optimization adjustments. After that, most clients either maintain the site themselves (we train you) or engage us on a monthly maintenance retainer. We also offer ongoing SEO and conversion rate optimization services.

Can you work with our existing brand, or do we need to start from scratch?

We work with what you have if it's solid, and we'll tell you honestly if we think a brand refresh would help. Many clients come with a logo and color scheme they love — we build around that. For clients with no existing brand or a brand that's working against them, we include brand identity as part of the engagement.
