What We Do

Acquiring a new customer costs significantly more than retaining an existing one. Most small businesses understand this intellectually but invest the majority of their energy in acquisition and very little in the deliberate design of what happens after someone becomes a client. The result is a business that works hard to fill the top of the funnel while quietly losing ground at the bottom through inconsistent delivery, poor onboarding, and missed opportunities to strengthen the relationship over time.

Customer journey mapping is where we start. We document every interaction a customer has with your business — from the moment they first hear about you through the entire lifecycle of the relationship. We look at what the experience feels like from the customer's perspective at each stage, where expectations are not met, where communication falls through, and where the business has opportunities to create memorable moments that generate loyalty and referrals. Most businesses are surprised by what this exercise reveals.

Onboarding improvement is often one of the highest-return investments a service business can make. The first 30 to 60 days of a client relationship set the tone for everything that follows. A structured, proactive onboarding process that sets clear expectations, delivers early wins, communicates regularly, and makes the client feel informed and valued dramatically reduces early churn and increases lifetime value. We design onboarding sequences that are both systematic enough to run consistently and personal enough to make clients feel genuinely cared for.

Retention systems address what happens in the middle and later stages of the client relationship. Many businesses do a reasonable job onboarding but then go quiet — clients hear from them only when there is a problem or a renewal conversation. We build proactive retention systems that include regular check-ins, milestone recognition, value delivery touchpoints, and early warning signals that tell you when a client's engagement is declining before it becomes a cancellation conversation.

Referral systems are among the most cost-effective growth levers available to small businesses, and yet most businesses leave referrals entirely to chance. We build structured referral programs that identify the right moment to ask, make the ask easy and natural, provide clients with the language and materials they need to refer effectively, and track the results so you can optimize the program over time. A well-designed referral system turns satisfied clients into an active growth channel without requiring additional marketing spend.

Who This Is For
  • Service businesses with high client acquisition cost that need longer client lifetimes to be profitable
  • Companies experiencing churn they cannot fully explain — clients leaving without a clear reason
  • Businesses that rely on word of mouth but have no structured process for generating referrals
  • Owners whose onboarding is inconsistent depending on who is handling the new client
  • Companies that have received negative feedback about the client experience but are not sure where to fix it
  • Businesses with good service delivery but weak communication between delivery milestones
  • Growing companies that need to maintain service quality as client volume increases

We work with clients across all 50 states. Customer experience engagements involve a combination of discovery calls to understand your current client journey, working sessions to redesign and document the improved process, and implementation support to make sure the new systems are actually embedded in how your team operates.
